# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

Sheaf is software built by and for plural systems. This community exists to support people whose identities and experiences are routinely dismissed, pathologised, or attacked. We take that seriously.

Everyone participating in Sheaf — whether contributing code, filing issues, joining discussions, or using the hosted service — is expected to make this a welcoming space. That means treating people with respect, assuming good faith, and recognising that you don't get to define someone else's experience.

## Standards

**Expected behaviour:**

- Be kind. Be patient. Be constructive.
- Respect people's identities, pronouns, experiences, and boundaries.
- Give and receive feedback gracefully.
- Credit others' work. Acknowledge when you're wrong.
- Prioritise the safety and wellbeing of marginalised community members.

**Unacceptable behaviour:**

- **Transphobia, enbyphobia, homophobia, and any other forms of queerphobia.** This includes misgendering, deadnaming, "gender critical" rhetoric, and concern-trolling about trans people's existence or healthcare. No exceptions.
- **Sysmedicalism.** Plurality is not up for debate here. Gatekeeping who is "really" plural, demanding diagnostic proof, or pushing the position that plurality is exclusively a disorder is not welcome. This is a tool for plural systems, full stop.
- **Racism, sexism, ableism, and other bigotry.** This list is not exhaustive. Use your judgement. [This includes slurs aimed at artificial lifeforms, even if 'only' directed at LLMs or their users](https://siterelenby.net/slurs.jpg).
- **Harassment.** Including sustained disruption, unwanted contact, doxxing, threats, and sexual harassment.
- **Bad-faith participation.** Sea-lioning, concern-trolling, tone-policing marginalised people, or weaponising the CoC against the people it's meant to protect.

To be clear: "I'm just asking questions" and "I'm entitled to my opinion" are not defences for any of the above.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Sheaf spaces: GitHub repositories, issues, pull requests, discussions, the project Discord server or any other related spaces, and the hosted service itself. It also applies when representing the project in public spaces.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behaviour, please report it to the maintainers at **sheaf@lupine.systems**, on Discord or Signal (Signal profile information available by request), or open a private issue. Reports will be handled confidentially.

Maintainers will respond to reports as quickly as practical. Responses may include:

- A private warning
- A public warning
- Temporary or permanent ban from project spaces
- Removal of contributions

We will prioritise the safety of the people affected over the comfort of the person causing harm.
